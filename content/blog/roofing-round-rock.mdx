## Why Round Rock Roofs Need Local Know-How

A roof in Round Rock works hard, and it's not because of one single factor — it's the combination.

**Hail is a recurring visitor.** Central Texas, including Round Rock, sits in a corridor that regularly sees hail events. Even minor hail can bruise shingles or dent metal panels in ways that aren't obvious from the ground but can shorten the roof's life.

**Wind follows the storms.** Straight-line winds and gusts from spring and summer storm systems can lift shingle edges and loosen flashing, especially on roofs that are already a few years past their prime.

**Summer heat never lets up.** Round Rock roofs face months of intense sun, and attic temperatures can climb well past what's comfortable. That heat accelerates shingle aging and makes proper attic ventilation important — something we assess on every inspection and dig into further in our [attic ventilation services](https://www.rippleroofs.com/services/attic-ventilation).

This is why we don't treat every inspection the same. A roof that's five years old after a hailstorm needs a different conversation than a roof that's twenty years old and simply worn out. Our [Round Rock roofing guide](https://www.rippleroofs.com/blog/round-rock-roofing-guide) covers more on what local homeowners should expect year to year.

### What does a roof replacement cost in Round Rock?

Cost depends on your roof's size, pitch, material choice, and any decking repairs needed. ### How long does a roof repair or replacement take?

Most repairs are completed in a single day. A standard shingle roof replacement usually takes one to two days; metal roofing projects and larger homes can take several days to a week depending on scope.

### Does Ripple Roofing offer warranties?

### What should I do after a hailstorm in Round Rock?

Avoid climbing on the roof yourself. Document any visible damage from the ground if it's safe to do so, and schedule a professional inspection promptly, since hail bruising isn't always visible right away and can worsen with the next rain.
