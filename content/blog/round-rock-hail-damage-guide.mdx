## Understanding Round Rock's Hail Season

### When Does Round Rock Get Hail?

Round Rock's **primary hail season runs from March through May**, with April being the most active month. During this period, warm Gulf moisture collides with cold fronts from the north, creating the perfect conditions for severe thunderstorms with large hail.

**Round Rock Hail Statistics:**
- **Average hail events per year:** 2-4 significant storms
- **Peak months:** March, April, May
- **Typical hail size:** Quarter to golf ball sized (1" to 1.75")
- **Severe storms:** Tennis ball or larger (2.5"+) every 3-5 years
- **Most affected neighborhoods:** Teravista, Walsh Ranch, Forest Creek, Heritage Center (northern Round Rock)

Round Rock's location at the intersection of I-35 and the Balcones Fault creates unique atmospheric conditions:

1. **Geographic positioning** — Situated where Hill Country meets flat prairie

## How to Identify Hail Damage on Your Round Rock Roof

Many homeowners miss hail damage because it's not always obvious from the ground. Here's what to look for after a Round Rock storm:

### Immediate Signs (Visible from Ground)

**Shingle damage:**
- Dark spots or bruises on shingles (exposed asphalt where granules knocked off)
- Dents in metal flashing, gutters, or vents
- Cracked or split shingles
- Missing shingles or loose shingle tabs

**Gutters and downspouts:**
- Dents on gutter edges or downspouts
- Paint chips on metal components
- Granules collecting in gutters (looks like coarse sand)

**Collateral damage indicators:**
- Dents in car hoods, air conditioning units, or mailboxes
- Damaged vinyl siding or window screens
- Broken skylights or damaged satellite dishes

### Hidden Damage (Requires Professional Inspection)

Professional roofers inspect for:
- **Fractured shingle seal strips** (invisible but compromises waterproofing)
- **Bruised decking underneath** (weakens structural integrity)
- **Compressed insulation** (reduces R-value and energy efficiency)
- **Micro-cracks in flashings** (won't leak immediately but will fail within 1-2 years)

**Don't assume your roof is fine** just because you don't see obvious damage. [Schedule a free professional inspection](/services/roof-inspection) within 72 hours of any hail event.

## The 72-Hour Window: Why Immediate Action Matters

After a Round Rock hailstorm, you have a critical **72-hour window** to protect your insurance claim:

### Hour 1-24: Document Everything

1. **Take photos and videos** from the ground (stay safe—don't climb on roof)
2. **Note the storm date and time** (check weather records if needed)
3. **Document collateral damage** (AC unit, gutters, siding, vehicles)
4. **Call your insurance company** to open a claim
5. **Request a professional inspection** from a local Round Rock roofer

### Hour 24-72: Get Professional Assessment

1. **Schedule inspections** with 2-3 licensed Round Rock roofing contractors
2. **Get detailed written estimates** with photo documentation
3. **Review your insurance policy** (replacement cost vs. actual cash value)
4. **Identify temporary repairs needed** to prevent further damage

### Why Speed Matters

- Insurance companies track storm dates—late claims raise red flags
- Secondary water damage from compromised shingles voids coverage
- Storm chasers flood the area—quality contractors book up fast
- HOA requirements may have strict timelines for visible damage

## Working with Insurance: Maximizing Your Round Rock Hail Claim

Most Round Rock homeowners are **entitled to a full roof replacement** when hail damage is properly documented. Here's how to ensure you get full coverage:

### What Your Insurance Should Cover

**Typical Round Rock hail claim coverage includes:**
- Complete roof replacement (if damage exceeds 8-10 squares or 25% of total roof)
- Matching shingles or full upgrade if discontinued
- Underlayment, decking repair, and ice & water shield
- Flashing, vents, and roof penetrations
- Gutter and downspout replacement
- Overhead and profit for contractor
- Dump fees and permit costs

**Your out-of-pocket cost:** Usually just your deductible ($2,500-$5,000 average in Round Rock)

### Common Insurance Tactics to Watch For

**1. "Cosmetic damage only" claim:**
Insurance adjusters may try to classify hail damage as "cosmetic" to deny claims. In Texas, **functional damage includes**:
- Loss of granule protection (exposes asphalt to UV)
- Compromised shingle seal strips (allows wind uplift)
- Fractured fiberglass mat (reduces lifespan by 50%+)

**Push back firmly** — hail damage is never purely cosmetic.

**2. "Repair only" instead of replacement:**
If your roof has 10+ damaged areas, **partial repairs void your warranty** and create a patchwork roof. Demand full replacement when warranted.

**3. Low square footage calculations:**
Some adjusters underestimate roof size. Get an independent measurement from your contractor.

**4. Depreciation (ACV vs. RCV):**
- **ACV (Actual Cash Value):** Depreciated amount (low payout)
- **RCV (Replacement Cost Value):** Full replacement cost (what you want)

Most policies cover RCV but pay ACV upfront, then reimburse depreciation after work completes. **Never let insurance pay you directly** — this can complicate final reimbursement.

### Working with Your Adjuster: Round Rock Tips

**Before the adjuster visits:**
- Get your own inspection from a [trusted Round Rock roofer](/roofing-round-rock)
- Have photos and documentation ready
- Know your policy coverage details

**During the inspection:**
- Be present but let professionals talk
- Point out ALL damaged areas (gutters, vents, AC)
- Request written estimate on-site
- Get adjuster's contact info and claim number

**If claim is denied or lowballed:**
- Request a re-inspection with your contractor present
- File a complaint with Texas Department of Insurance
- Consider hiring a public adjuster (they work for you, not insurance company)
- Get a second opinion from another contractor

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How long do I have to file a hail damage claim in Round Rock?**

A: Texas law requires insurance companies to accept claims filed within the policy period, but most policies have a 1-year deadline from the date of loss. File as soon as possible after discovering damage—ideally within 72 hours of the storm.

**Q: Can I get a full roof replacement if only part is damaged?**

A: Yes. If hail damage affects 8-10 squares (800-1,000 sq ft) or 25%+ of your roof, insurance typically covers full replacement. Texas law also requires matching shingles—if yours are discontinued, the entire roof must be replaced with new materials.

**Q: Will filing a hail claim raise my insurance rates?**

A: Not in most cases. Texas considers hail damage a "no-fault" claim (act of God). However, multiple claims within 3-5 years can affect future insurability. Check with your insurance agent.

**Q: How much does hail damage roof replacement cost in Round Rock?**

A: Average cost is $8,000-$15,000 for a typical 2,000 sq ft Round Rock home with architectural shingles. With insurance, you typically pay only your deductible ($2,500-$5,000). Upgrading to Class 4 shingles adds $1,500-$3,000.

**Q: Should I get multiple estimates after Round Rock hail damage?**

A: Yes. Get 2-3 written estimates from licensed Round Rock roofing contractors. Compare scope of work (not just price), warranties, and insurance claim experience. Avoid the lowest bid—it often means shortcuts or storm chasers.

**Q: How long does a Round Rock roof replacement take?**

A: Most residential roof replacements take 1-3 days depending on size and complexity. Your home is protected and watertight at the end of each workday. Full project including insurance approval and scheduling typically takes 4-8 weeks.

**Q: What if my insurance denies my Round Rock hail claim?**

A: Request a re-inspection with your roofing contractor present. Document all damage thoroughly. File a complaint with the Texas Department of Insurance if needed. Consider hiring a public adjuster who works for you (not the insurance company).
