## Factors That Determine Your Roof Replacement Cost

### 1. Roof Size (Square Footage)

Roofing contractors measure in 'squares' (100 square feet). A 2,000 sq ft home typically has 2,200-2,400 sq ft of roof surface (accounting for pitch/slope).

**San Antonio Home Size Breakdown:**
| Home Size | Roof Squares | Typical Cost Range |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1,200-1,500 sq ft | 14-18 squares | $10,500-$15,000 |
| 1,500-2,000 sq ft | 18-24 squares | $13,000-$19,000 |
| 2,000-2,500 sq ft | 24-30 squares | $16,000-$24,000 |
| 2,500-3,000 sq ft | 30-36 squares | $20,000-$28,000 |
| 3,000-4,000 sq ft | 36-48 squares | $25,000-$38,000 |
| 4,000+ sq ft | 48+ squares | $35,000-$55,000+ |

**Note:** These are for standard architectural shingles. Impact-resistant and metal roofs cost more.

### 2. Roofing Material Type

**Architectural Shingles (Most Common):**
- **Cost**: $350-$500 per square installed
- **Lifespan**: 18-25 years in San Antonio heat
- **Pros**: Affordable, widely available, good variety
- **Cons**: Shorter lifespan in extreme heat, vulnerable to hail

**Impact-Resistant Shingles (Highly Recommended):**
- **Cost**: $450-$650 per square installed
- **Lifespan**: 25-30 years
- **Pros**: Class 4 hail rating, insurance discounts (20-35%), better heat resistance
- **Cons**: Higher upfront cost
- **ROI**: Insurance savings typically recover cost difference in 5-7 years

**Metal Roofing (Premium):**
- **Cost**: $800-$1,400 per square installed
- **Lifespan**: 40-70 years
- **Pros**: Longest lasting, reflects heat (lower cooling bills 15-25%), hail resistant, fire resistant
- **Cons**: 2-3x cost of shingles, louder in rain, limited contractor expertise

**Tile Roofing (Luxury/Historic):**
- **Cost**: $1,000-$1,800 per square installed
- **Lifespan**: 50+ years
- **Pros**: Beautiful, extremely durable, fire resistant
- **Cons**: Very heavy (structural upgrades often needed), expensive, limited to certain architectural styles

### 3. Roof Pitch/Complexity

**Low-Slope (4/12 or less):**
- Easier to work on, faster installation
- Standard labor rates apply

**Steep Slope (8/12 or higher):**
- Requires additional safety equipment
- Slower installation (more dangerous)
- Labor premium: +$50-$100 per square

**Complex Roofs:**
- Multiple valleys, dormers, skylights
- Turrets, irregular shapes
- Add 15-30% to base cost

### 4. Current Roof Condition

**Decking in Good Shape:**
- Standard tear-off and replacement
- Base pricing applies

**Damaged Decking:**
- Rot, water damage, sagging
- Decking replacement: **$75-$150 per sheet** (4x8 ft)
- Average homes need 3-8 sheets replaced: **$500-$1,500 extra**
- Severe cases (complete re-deck): **+$3,000-$8,000**

**Multiple Layers:**
- San Antonio building code allows max 2 layers
- Extra tear-off layer: **+$1,500-$3,000**
- Disposal fees increase with weight

### 5. Removal & Disposal

**Standard Tear-Off:**
- Included in most quotes
- Typical: $100-$150 per square for tear-off + disposal

**Heavy Materials:**
- Tile or slate removal: **+$2,000-$5,000**
- Multiple shingle layers: **+$1,000-$2,500**

**Dumpster/Debris:**
- Usually included in quotes
- Verify this‚Äîsome contractors charge separately

### 6. Additional Components

**Underlayment:**
- Synthetic underlayment (recommended): **$60-$100 per square**
- Basic felt underlayment: **$40-$60 per square**
- Ice & water shield (valleys, eaves): **+$200-$600**

**Flashing:**
- Chimney flashing: **$400-$800**
- Skylight flashing: **$300-$600 per skylight**
- Valley flashing (metal): **$150-$300 per valley**
- Drip edge: **$3-$5 per linear foot** (typically $300-$600 total)

**Ventilation:**
- Ridge vent: **$6-$10 per linear foot** ($400-$800 total)
- Roof vents: **$100-$200 each** (need 2-4 typically)
- Attic fans: **$400-$1,200** if upgrading

**Gutters:**
- Usually separate from roof replacement
- 6' seamless aluminum gutters: **$8-$12 per linear foot**
- Average home: **$1,200-$2,500 for gutters + downspouts**

## Ways to Save Money on Roof Replacement

### 1. File Insurance Claim for Storm Damage

**Biggest Money Saver:**
If you have legitimate hail or wind damage from recent storms, insurance should cover replacement. 

**What's Covered:**
- Hail damage (circular impacts, granule loss)
- Wind damage (missing/torn shingles)
- Tree damage from storms
- Typically full replacement if damage is widespread

**What's NOT Covered:**
- Normal wear and tear
- Age-related deterioration
- Poor maintenance
- Cosmetic issues only

**San Antonio Tip:** Check storm dates against your roof condition. Major hail events in 2024: Feb 28, April 9, May 15, Oct 12. If your roof looks damaged and dates match, file a claim.

### 2. Choose Off-Peak Season

**Best Times:**
- **Fall** (October-November): Ideal weather, less demand
- **Winter** (December-February): Slowest season, best discounts (10-20% off)

**Avoid:**

## Insurance Claims: What San Antonio Homeowners Need to Know

### Texas-Specific Rules

**1-Year Filing Deadline:**
You must file insurance claims within **1 year of storm date** in Texas. Document every major hail/wind event.

**Statute of Limitations:**
You have up to 2 years to file lawsuit if claim is unfairly denied, but best to file claim within 1 year.

### Common Insurance Issues in San Antonio

**Claim Denials:**
- 'Cosmetic damage only' (hail dents but no functional impact)
- 'Wear and tear' (even if storm damage exists)
- 'Wind-driven rain' exclusion (leak claims)
- 'Matching shingles not covered' (discontinued colors)

**How to Fight Denials:**
1. Get independent inspection from certified roofer
2. Provide documentation: photos, storm dates, expert opinion
3. File supplement with additional evidence
4. Hire public adjuster if needed (10-15% of settlement)
5. File complaint with Texas Department of Insurance

### Typical Settlement Process

**Timeline:**
1. **File claim:** Same day as damage discovery
2. **Adjuster visit:** 3-14 days
3. **Initial estimate:** 7-21 days
4. **Negotiate/supplement:** 1-4 weeks
5. **Final approval:** 2-8 weeks total

**Payment:**
- Usually 2 payments: **ACV (Actual Cash Value)** upfront, **RCV (Replacement Cost Value)** after completion
- **Deductible:** $1,000-$2,500 typical (you pay this)
- **Example:** $20,000 roof, $1,500 deductible
  - Insurance ACV payment: $12,000 (depreciated value)
  - You pay: $1,500 deductible + difference until completion
  - Insurance RCV payment: $6,500 after work complete
  - **Your total cost: $1,500**

## San Antonio Roof Replacement: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much does a new roof cost in San Antonio?**
A: Average San Antonio roof replacement costs $12,500-$26,000 depending on home size and materials. Standard 2,000 sq ft home with architectural shingles: $13,000-$18,000. Impact-resistant shingles: $15,500-$22,000. Metal roofing: $26,000-$45,000.

**Q: Will insurance pay for my new roof?**
A: Insurance covers roof replacement if you have storm damage (hail, wind, fallen trees) from recent events. Normal wear and tear is not covered. Must file claim within 1 year of storm date in Texas. Deductible applies ($1,000-$2,500 typical).

**Q: How long does roof replacement take?**
A: Most San Antonio homes take 1-3 days. Standard 2,000 sq ft home: 1 day for shingles, 2-3 days for metal roofing. Larger/complex roofs: 3-5 days. Weather delays can extend timeline.

**Q: What's the best time to replace a roof in San Antonio?**
A: Fall (October-November) and winter (December-February) are ideal‚Äîcooler temperatures, better shingle adhesion, lower demand means better pricing (save 10-20%). Avoid summer peak season if possible.

**Q: Should I get impact-resistant shingles?**
A: Highly recommended for San Antonio. Class 4 impact-resistant shingles cost $3,000-$5,000 more but provide 20-35% insurance discounts. Payback period: 5-7 years, then you save money every year. Better hail protection too.

**Q: Can I reroof over existing shingles?**
A: Texas allows maximum 2 layers. If you have 1 layer, you *can* reroof over, but not recommended‚Äîhidden damage, reduced lifespan, heavier load. Most contractors recommend complete tear-off for longest-lasting results.

**Q: How do I know if I have hail damage?**
A: Look for circular dents/bruises on shingles, granule loss (black spots showing through), cracked shingles, dented vents/gutters. Use binoculars from ground‚Äîdon't climb on roof. Get professional inspection (free from us).

**Q: What's the difference between architectural and 3-tab shingles?**
A: Architectural shingles are thicker, more durable, better looking, longer lasting (25-30 vs. 15-20 years). Cost about 30% more but worth it. 3-tab shingles are largely obsolete‚Äî90% of new roofs use architectural.

**Q: Do I need a permit for roof replacement in San Antonio?**
A: Yes, permit required for full roof replacement ($150-$400). Legitimate contractors pull permits‚Äîit's included in their quote. Permits ensure code compliance and protect you.

**Q: How often should I replace my roof in San Antonio?**
A: Architectural shingles: 18-25 years. Impact-resistant shingles: 25-30 years. Metal roofing: 40-70 years. San Antonio's extreme heat shortens lifespan vs. cooler climates. Plan replacement at 20 years for shingles.
