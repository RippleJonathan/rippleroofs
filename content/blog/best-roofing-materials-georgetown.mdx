## Historic District Material Requirements

If your property is in Georgetown's Historic District (roughly downtown, bordered by Church/8th/Main/Austin), you need Georgetown Historic Preservation Commission (GHPC) approval for roofing changes.

### GHPC-Approved Materials

**‚úÖ Usually Approved:**
- Class 4 impact shingles in period-appropriate colors (gray, black, brown, weathered wood)
- Dimensional shingles matching original profile
- Standing seam metal (if historically accurate for property)
- Clay or concrete tile (Spanish/Mediterranean styles)
- Cedar shake appearance (synthetic allowed if realistic)

**‚ùå Usually Denied:**
- Bright or unusual colors (blues, greens, reds on non-Spanish homes)
- Modern 'architectural' profiles on Victorian homes
- Shiny metal finishes (chrome, bright galvanized)
- Inconsistent materials (mixing styles)

### Application Process

1. **Consult Contractor:** Get material recommendations from Georgetown-experienced roofer
2. **Submit Application:** Include photos, material samples, color chips, contractor info
3. **Wait for Review:** 2-4 weeks typical (can be expedited for emergency repairs)
4. **Receive Certificate of Appropriateness:** Required before starting work
5. **Proceed with Installation:** Must follow approved plan

**Application Fee:** $75-$200 depending on project scope

**Georgetown Pro Tip:** Submit applications with 'storm damage emergency' notation for faster review. GHPC prioritizes weather-damaged roofs.

### Recommended Historic Materials

**For Victorian/Traditional Homes:**
- CertainTeed Landmark IR in Weathered Wood, Pewter, or Charcoal
- Certainteed Presidential Shake IR (dimensional profile)
- GAF Timberline HDZ RS in Charcoal or Weathered Wood

**For Spanish/Mediterranean Homes:**
- Concrete tile in terracotta or mission red
- Clay tile (if structurally feasible)
- Standing seam metal in medium bronze or copper
