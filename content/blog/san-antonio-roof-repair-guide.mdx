## San Antonio's Unique Roofing Challenges

### Climate Factors That Damage Roofs

**Extreme Heat:**
- Summer temperatures consistently exceed 100¬∞F
- UV exposure degrades shingles faster than cooler climates
- Thermal cycling (hot days, cooler nights) causes expansion/contraction damage
- Asphalt shingles typically last 15-20 years vs. 25-30 in cooler climates

**Severe Hail Events:**
- San Antonio sits in "Hail Alley"‚Äîaveraging 3-5 significant hail events per year
- Golf ball to baseball-sized hail common in spring storms (March-May)

**Wind Damage:**
- Severe thunderstorms with 60-80 mph straight-line winds
- Lift and tear shingles, especially on older roofs with degraded sealant
- Tree damage from oaks and pecans common in older neighborhoods

**Heavy Rain & Flash Flooding:**
- Intense rainfall overwhelms drainage systems
- Poor attic ventilation leads to moisture damage
- Flat/low-slope roofs in commercial areas prone to ponding water

## Common Roof Repair Issues in San Antonio

### 1. Hail Damage (Most Common)

**What It Looks Like:**
- Circular bruising/dents on shingle surfaces
- Granule loss exposing black asphalt underneath
- Cracked or split shingles
- Damaged flashing, vents, and skylights
- Dented gutters and downspouts

**Typical Costs:**
- Minor hail damage repair: $2,500-$5,000
- Moderate damage (partial roof sections): $5,000-$12,000
- Severe damage requiring full replacement: $15,000-$28,000

**Insurance Coverage:**
Most homeowners insurance policies cover hail damage. Key: file claim within 1 year of storm date (Texas statute). Don't wait‚Äîdamage worsens with every rain.

### 2. Heat-Related Shingle Deterioration

**What It Looks Like:**
- Curling or cupping shingles
- Cracked/brittle shingles that break easily
- Missing granules (roof looks "bald")
- Blistering or bubbling surface

**Typical Costs:**
- Shingle replacement (small section): $800-$2,500
- Multiple sections: $3,000-$8,000
- Preventive re-roofing: $12,000-$25,000

**Fix:** Upgrade to Class 4 impact-resistant shingles with high reflectivity ratings (cool roof shingles) that resist heat damage and lower cooling costs by 10-15%.

### 3. Leak Repairs

**Common Sources:**
- Damaged flashing (chimneys, skylights, valleys)
- Worn pipe boot seals
- Missing or damaged shingles
- Ice dam damage (rare but happens in occasional hard freezes)

**Typical Costs:**
- Simple flashing repair: $400-$900
- Pipe boot replacement: $300-$600
- Multiple leak sources: $1,200-$3,500
- Emergency tarping after storm: $350-$700

**Warning Signs:**
- Water stains on ceiling
- Musty attic smell
- Peeling interior paint
- Visible daylight through roof boards

### 4. Wind Damage

**What It Looks Like:**
- Missing shingles (especially ridge caps)
- Lifted shingle tabs
- Exposed nail heads
- Torn or creased shingles
- Granule-filled gutters

**Typical Costs:**
- Minor wind damage (few shingles): $600-$1,500
- Moderate damage (multiple areas): $2,000-$5,000
- Severe damage with decking repair: $5,000-$12,000

**Note:** Wind damage often accompanies hail‚Äîinspect entire roof if either is present.

### 5. Tree Damage

**What It Looks Like:**
- Puncture holes from falling branches
- Scrape marks from overhanging limbs
- Clogged gutters with leaves/debris
- Moss or algae growth from shade/moisture

**Typical Costs:**
- Branch removal + minor repair: $800-$2,000
- Significant tree damage: $3,000-$8,000
- Emergency tree removal + tarping: $1,500-$4,000

**Prevention:** Trim oak and pecan trees annually. Overhanging branches should be 6+ feet from roof surface.

## Filing Insurance Claims for Roof Repairs in San Antonio

### Step 1: Document Damage Immediately

**Within 24 Hours of Storm:**
1. Take photos/video from ground level (DO NOT climb on roof)
2. Document interior damage (water stains, leaks)
3. Note date/time of storm event
4. Check neighbors‚Äîhail damage is usually widespread

**Key Documentation:**
- Close-up photos of hail impacts on shingles
- Wide shots showing overall roof condition
- Photos of gutters, vents, skylights (often damaged too)
- Interior water damage photos
- Storm date confirmation (NOAA weather reports)

### Step 2: Call Your Insurance Company

**Critical Timing:**
- File claim within 1 year of storm date (Texas law)
- Earlier is better‚Äîinsurers track storm dates
- Don't wait for multiple storms (hard to prove which caused damage)

**What to Say:**
- "I have storm damage to my roof from [date]"
- Request full roof inspection, not just visible areas
- Ask about coverage for matching shingles (discontinued colors)
- Confirm deductible amount

### Step 3: Get Professional Inspection (Before Adjuster)

**Why This Matters:**
Insurance adjusters work for the insurance company‚Äînot you. Having independent documentation strengthens your claim.

**What We Provide:**

- Detailed damage report with measurements
- Professional photos for claim documentation
- Estimate for repairs/replacement

**Cost:** FREE for insurance inspections (we only get paid if claim approved)

### Step 4: Meet With Insurance Adjuster

**Tips for Adjuster Meeting:**
- Be present during inspection
- Have your contractor there (we attend for free)
- Point out ALL damage areas
- Ask adjuster to test shingles (brittle/cracked from hail)
- Request written estimate on-site

**Common Adjuster Tactics:**
- Claiming damage is "wear and tear" not storm damage
- Only approving partial repairs when full replacement needed
- Lowball initial estimate
- Denying claim for "cosmetic damage only"

**Our Role:** We advocate for you, ensuring adjuster sees all damage and provides fair estimate.

### Step 5: Review Settlement Offer

**Check For:**
- ‚úÖ Full roof replacement vs. partial repair

- ‚úÖ Decking repair included

- ‚úÖ Reasonable labor rates

**Red Flags:**
- ‚ùå Settlement way below contractor estimates
- ‚ùå "Roof repair" when replacement clearly needed
- ‚ùå Excluding obvious damaged areas
- ‚ùå Depreciation reducing payout unfairly

### Step 6: Get Second Opinion If Claim Denied

**If Insurance Denies Claim:**
1. Request written denial with specific reasons
2. Get independent inspection from certified contractor
3. File supplement with additional documentation
4. Consider public adjuster (10-15% of claim but often worth it)
5. Know your rights‚Äîfile complaint with Texas Department of Insurance if needed

## San Antonio Roof Repair: Frequently Asked Questions

**Q: How much does roof repair cost in San Antonio?**
A: Average roof repairs in San Antonio range from $800-$5,000 depending on damage extent. Minor leak repairs ($400-$1,200), moderate shingle replacement ($2,000-$4,000), and major hail damage repairs ($5,000-$12,000) are most common.

**Q: Will insurance cover my roof repair?**
A: Most homeowners insurance covers storm damage (hail, wind, falling trees). Must file claim within 1 year of storm date in Texas. Normal wear and tear is typically not covered.

**Q: How do I know if I need repair or replacement?**
A: **Q: When should I repair vs. replace my San Antonio roof?**
A: If your roof is &lt;10 years old with localized damage, repair usually makes sense. If 15+ years old with widespread damage, replacement is typically more cost-effective. Get professional inspection for honest assessment.

**Q: How long does roof repair take in San Antonio?**
A: Minor repairs (leaks, small shingle sections) take 2-6 hours. Moderate repairs (multiple areas) take 1-2 days. Major hail damage repairs take 2-4 days depending on extent.

**Q: Should I repair my roof in summer or winter?**
A: In San Antonio, fall and winter (October-February) are ideal‚Äîcooler temperatures, less rain, better shingle adhesion. Avoid summer repairs when possible (extreme heat makes work difficult and shingles fragile).

**Q: How can I tell if my roof has hail damage?**
A: Look for circular dents/bruises on shingles, granule loss (black spots), cracked shingles, dented gutters/vents. From ground, use binoculars. NEVER climb on roof yourself‚Äîcall professional inspector.

**Q: What's the difference between a roofing company and a storm chaser?**
A: Legitimate roofing companies have local addresses, Texas licenses, years of local business history, and local references. Storm chasers appear after storms, pressure immediate signing, have no local presence, and often disappear after payment.

**Q: Do I need a permit for roof repair in San Antonio?**
A: Minor repairs (small areas, flashing) typically don't require permits. Major repairs or full replacement require permits. Licensed contractors handle permitting.

**Q: How often should I inspect my roof in San Antonio?**
A: At minimum, inspect annually and after every major storm. San Antonio's severe weather warrants inspections after hail events, high winds (&gt;50 mph), or if you notice any interior water damage signs.

**Q: Can I do roof repairs myself?**
A: Not recommended. Roofing is dangerous (falls are #1 construction injury), requires specialized tools/knowledge, and DIY repairs often void manufacturer warranties. Most importantly, improperly sealed repairs lead to leaks causing thousands in interior damage.
