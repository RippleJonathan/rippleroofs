## Why Georgetown Homes Need Texas-Tough Roofing

Georgetown roofs work hard. Summer heat can push attic temperatures well above 120°F on hot days, which over time can wear down lower-quality shingles faster than in milder climates. Then spring and early summer bring hailstorms that roll through Williamson County, sometimes with little warning. Add in strong straight-line winds, and it's easy to see why a roof built for a milder climate just doesn't hold up here.

That's why we recommend materials rated for impact resistance and heat performance, not just whatever's on the shelf. A roof that's built for Georgetown's climate protects your home better and typically lasts longer between major repairs. If you're weighing your options, our [material comparison chart](https://www.rippleroofs.com/resources/material-comparison-chart), [material comparison tool](https://www.rippleroofs.com/resources/material-comparison-tool), and [best roofing materials for Georgetown](https://www.rippleroofs.com/blog/best-roofing-materials-georgetown) guide are good starting points.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How much does a roof replacement cost in Georgetown?

Costs vary based on roof size, pitch, and material choice — asphalt shingles, metal, and stone-coated steel all come in at different price points. We provide a detailed, itemized estimate after an in-person inspection so you know exactly what you're paying for.

### How long does a typical roof last in Georgetown's climate?

A well-installed asphalt shingle roof commonly lasts around 20-25 years in Central Texas, while metal roofing can last significantly longer. Heat, hail, and UV exposure all affect lifespan, which is why material choice matters.

### Do you offer emergency roof repair after a storm?

Yes. If a storm has caused visible damage or an active leak, we offer emergency roofing services to help stabilize your home quickly.

### How do I know if I need a repair or a full replacement?

It depends on the extent and age of the damage. A few missing shingles or a small leak often just needs a repair, while widespread hail damage or a roof nearing the end of its lifespan usually calls for replacement.
