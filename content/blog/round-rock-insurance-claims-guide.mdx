## What Round Rock Insurance Companies Actually Cover

### Covered Perils (What They WILL Pay For)

‚úÖ **Hail Damage**
- Granule loss exposing asphalt substrate
- Fractured or cracked shingles
- Bruised/dented shingles (functional damage)
- Damaged flashing, vents, gutters, downspouts
- Broken roof penetrations (skylights, pipe boots)

‚úÖ **Wind Damage**
- Missing shingles from high winds
- Lifted or curled shingles
- Torn or displaced flashing
- Damaged underlayment exposure
- Blown-off ridge caps

‚úÖ **Combined Storm Damage**
- Hail + wind in same storm event
- Fallen tree limbs from storm
- Debris impact during severe weather
- Ice dam damage (from winter storms)

‚úÖ **Full Replacement When**:
- Damage exceeds 40% of roof (most policies)
- Matching shingles unavailable (discontinued colors)
- Structural damage to decking
- Multiple storm events create cumulative damage

### NOT Covered (What They WON'T Pay For)

‚ùå **Normal Wear and Tear**
- Age-related deterioration
- UV degradation over time
- Granule loss from normal weathering
- Manufacturing defects (file with manufacturer)

‚ùå **Pre-Existing Damage**
- Damage from storms BEFORE your policy started
- Prior damage you didn't report
- Neglected repairs from past storms

‚ùå **Maintenance Issues**
- Lack of preventive maintenance
- Clogged gutters causing water damage
- Poor ventilation leading to premature failure
- DIY repairs done incorrectly

‚ùå **Cosmetic Damage Only** (Some Policies)
- **CRITICAL**: Some Round Rock policies have "cosmetic damage exclusions"
- Means hail dents that don't affect function may not be covered
- Check your policy declarations page
- Most Round Rock homeowners DON'T have these exclusions

### Round Rock-Specific Policy Considerations

**Typical Round Rock Deductibles**:
- **Percentage-Based**: 1-2% of dwelling coverage
  - $300,000 home = $3,000-$6,000 deductible
  - $400,000 home = $4,000-$8,000 deductible
- **Flat Deductible**: $2,500-$5,000 (less common)
- **Separate Wind/Hail**: Sometimes higher deductible just for wind/hail (check policy)

**Replacement Cost Value (RCV) vs Actual Cash Value (ACV)**:
- **RCV**: Insurance pays full replacement cost (what most Round Rock policies have)
  - Example: New roof costs $18,000, you pay $3,000 deductible, insurance pays $15,000
- **ACV**: Insurance pays depreciated value (rare but check your policy)
  - Example: 15-year-old roof worth $10,000 depreciated, insurance pays $7,000 after deductible
  - You're stuck with $11,000 out-of-pocket

**How Payment Works**:
1. **Initial Payment (ACV)**: Insurance pays depreciated amount upfront
   - Example: $18,000 claim, 20% depreciation = $14,400 initial check
2. **Final Payment (Recoverable Depreciation)**: After work completed, you submit invoice
   - Example: Submit $18,000 invoice, insurance pays remaining $3,600
3. **You Pay**: Your deductible ($3,000) at time of work completion

## Common Round Rock Claim Denials (And How to Fight Them)

### Denial Reason #1: "Pre-Existing Damage"

**What They Say**:  
*"This damage existed before the storm event. Claim denied."*

**How to Fight It**:
- ‚úÖ Provide storm date and National Weather Service hail reports
- ‚úÖ Show photos of neighbors' damage from same storm
- ‚úÖ Get expert opinion from contractor (letter stating damage is storm-related)
- ‚úÖ Reference recent roof inspection (if you had one pre-storm showing no damage)
- ‚úÖ Appeal with documentation proving damage timeline

**Round Rock Example**: After March 2023 hailstorm, some adjusters claimed damage was "from the 2021 storm." Homeowners fought back with photos taken AFTER 2021 storm showing no damage, plus weather service confirmation of March 2023 golf ball hail. Most denials overturned.

### Denial Reason #2: "Cosmetic Damage Only"

**What They Say**:  
*"We see hail dents, but they're cosmetic. Your policy excludes cosmetic damage."*

**How to Fight It**:
- ‚úÖ Review your policy‚Äîmost Round Rock homeowners DON'T have cosmetic exclusions
- ‚úÖ Get contractor letter explaining functional damage (granule loss = shortened lifespan = functional)
- ‚úÖ Document that matching shingles aren't available (aesthetic match impossible = functional issue)
- ‚úÖ Cite Texas Insurance Code requirements for proper coverage

**Key Argument**: Hail damage that exposes asphalt substrate or fractures shingle seal strips is FUNCTIONAL damage, not cosmetic. Cosmetic = purely aesthetic with no impact on performance.

### Denial Reason #3: "Roof Too Old / Normal Wear"

**What They Say**:  
*"Your roof is 15 years old. This is normal wear and tear, not storm damage."*

**How to Fight It**:
- ‚úÖ Confirm you have RCV (Replacement Cost Value) coverage, not ACV
- ‚úÖ RCV policies cover storm damage regardless of age
- ‚úÖ Get before-and-after documentation (storm created NEW damage, not wear)
- ‚úÖ Expert opinion: Contractor explains this is storm damage, not age-related failure
- ‚úÖ Texas law: Age doesn't void coverage for covered perils

**Round Rock Reality**: Many Round Rock roofs are 10-20 years old due to repeated hailstorms replacing roofs in cycles. Age doesn't matter if you have RCV coverage and can prove storm damage.

### Denial Reason #4: "Not Enough Damage"

**What They Say**:  
*"We only found 3 hail impacts. Not enough to warrant replacement."*

**How to Fight It**:
- ‚úÖ Your contractor likely found MORE damage (have them document every impact)
- ‚úÖ Request adjuster re-inspect with YOUR contractor present
- ‚úÖ Cite policy‚Äîmost don't have "minimum impact" threshold
- ‚úÖ Argue: Even limited impacts on critical areas (valleys, penetrations) = functional damage
- ‚úÖ If shingles discontinued, entire roof must be replaced for match

**Counter-Argument**: "My policy doesn't specify a threshold for approval. Hail damage to ANY portion of the roof compromises its integrity and warranty. Additionally, matching my discontinued shingles for a partial repair is impossible, requiring full replacement per policy provisions."

## Frequently Asked Questions

**How long do I have to file a claim after a Round Rock hailstorm?**

Most policies allow 1 year from date of loss, but file within 7-14 days for best results. After major Round Rock hailstorms, adjusters get backlogged‚Äîearly filing = faster service.

**Will my insurance rates go up if I file a claim?**

Typically no. Hail is a "no-fault" natural disaster. However, check with your agent. Filing multiple claims in 3-5 years MAY affect rates or insurability. But don't let fear of rate increases stop you from filing legitimate claims‚Äîthat's why you pay premiums.

**What if my adjuster says I don't have enough damage?**

Request a second inspection with YOUR contractor present. Get written documentation from contractor. File a formal appeal with photos and expert opinion. If still denied unfairly, hire public adjuster or consult insurance attorney.

**Can I choose my own contractor, or do I have to use insurance company's preferred vendor?**

YOU choose your contractor. Insurance cannot require you to use their "preferred vendor" list. Those contractors may cut corners to meet insurance pricing. Choose based on quality, certifications (CertainTeed Shingle Master), and local reputation.

**What if my roof is 20 years old? Will insurance still pay?**

If you have Replacement Cost Value (RCV) coverage, yes. Age doesn't matter for storm damage claims. If you only have Actual Cash Value (ACV) coverage, they'll depreciate based on age. Check your policy declarations page.

**Should I tarp my roof after storm damage?**

If actively leaking, yes‚Äîtemporary tarp prevents further damage. Save receipts (insurance may reimburse tarping costs). But don't attempt yourself‚Äîhire professional to avoid injury and liability.

**How much will my deductible be?**

Check your policy. Most Round Rock homeowners have 1-2% of dwelling coverage:
- $300,000 home = $3,000-$6,000 deductible
- $400,000 home = $4,000-$8,000 deductible

Some policies have flat deductibles ($2,500, $5,000). Separate wind/hail deductibles sometimes higher.

**What's the difference between ACV and RCV payments?**

- **ACV (Actual Cash Value)**: Depreciated amount paid upfront
- **RCV (Replacement Cost Value)**: Full replacement cost (ACV + recoverable depreciation paid after work completed)

Most Round Rock policies are RCV. You receive ACV initially, submit invoice after completion, receive remaining depreciation.
