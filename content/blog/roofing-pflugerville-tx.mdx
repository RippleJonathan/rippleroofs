## Common Roofing Issues We See in Pflugerville

Pflugerville sits right in the path of Central Texas's toughest weather swings, and that shows up in the roofs we inspect around town.

**Heat and UV exposure.** Our summers push shingles hard, drying out asphalt and accelerating granule loss over time. Roofs here age faster than in milder climates, which is why regular inspections matter more here than in most parts of the country.

**Hail damage.** Pflugerville has seen its share of hailstorms roll through the I-35 corridor. Hail can bruise or crack shingles in ways that aren't obvious from the ground but lead to leaks months later. If a storm has come through your neighborhood recently, it's worth having a professional take a look before small damage turns into a bigger repair.

**High winds.** Spring storms in this area often bring strong straight-line winds that can lift shingle edges, loosen flashing, or tear off ridge caps — especially on older roofs that are already due for attention.

**Aging roof materials.** A lot of Pflugerville's growth happened in waves over the past couple decades, which means many neighborhoods now have roofs approaching the end of their expected lifespan around the same time. If your roof is 15–20+ years old, it's a good time to get an inspection even if nothing looks obviously wrong.

For more on how to prepare before storm season hits, check out our [Texas storm season preparation guide](https://www.rippleroofs.com/blog/texas-storm-season-preparation).
