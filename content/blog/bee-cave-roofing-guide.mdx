## Common Roofing Problems in Bee Cave

### 1. Hail Damage

**Central Texas Hail Alley**:
Bee Cave sits in one of Texas's most active hail zones. Major events in recent years:
- March 2024: Golf ball size hail, extensive damage
- May 2023: Quarter to half-dollar size, scattered damage
- April 2021: Baseball size in parts of Bee Cave
- October 2020: Quarter size, widespread
- April 2016: Softball size, catastrophic event

**Prevention and Response**:
- **Class 4 IR shingles**: Essential for Bee Cave
- **Post-storm inspection**: After every significant hail event
- **Document damage**: Photos for insurance
- **File claims promptly**: Don't delay‚ÄîTexas law protects your rights
- **Hire local experts**: Storm chasers disappear; local contractors stay

### 2. Wind Damage

**Hill Country Wind Exposure**:
- Elevated terrain increases wind speeds
- Spring and early summer storms (50-80+ mph gusts)
- Hilltop homes especially vulnerable
- Falling oak limbs during storms

**Prevention**:
- High wind-rated materials (130 mph minimum)
- Proper installation (critical for wind resistance)
- Oak tree trimming (10+ feet clearance)
- Regular inspections after storms

### 3. Oak Tree Challenges

**Beautiful But High-Maintenance**:
- Live oaks add tremendous value
- But create roofing challenges:
  - Falling leaves and acorns (gutter clogs)
  - Limb damage during storms
  - Shade retains moisture
  - Root systems can affect structures

**Best Practices**:
- Annual professional arborist assessment ($300-$1,500)
- Regular trimming (keep 10+ feet clearance)
- Gutter cleaning 3-4 times per year
- Balance preservation with protection
- Trim dead or diseased branches immediately

### 4. Flash Flood and Drainage

**Hill Country Terrain**:
- Steep slopes common in Bee Cave
- Flash flood potential during severe storms
- Proper drainage essential

**Roofing Considerations**:
- Gutters and downspouts critical
- Proper grading around foundation
- Roof drainage must be directed appropriately
- Valley flashing extra important

### 5. Heat and UV Degradation

**Intense Texas Sun**:
- 300+ sunny days per year
- Roof surface temps: 150-170¬∞F+
- UV exposure accelerates aging
- South/west facing slopes age faster

**Protection Strategies**:
- Cool roof technology (reflective granules)
- Light colors (reflect heat)
- Proper ventilation (reduces attic temps by 20-40¬∞F)
- Regular maintenance
- Quality materials last longer in heat

## Insurance Claims in Bee Cave

### Storm Damage Claims Process

**Step 1: Document Damage** (Immediately After Storm):
- Take extensive photos/video
- Note date and time of storm
- Document all visible damage
- Save weather reports showing hail size

**Step 2: File Claim Promptly**:
- Contact insurance within 48-72 hours
- Provide documentation
- Request prompt adjuster inspection

**Step 3: Get Professional Inspection**:
- Hire reputable local contractor for independent assessment
- Have contractor present during adjuster visit
- Get written estimate

**Step 4: Review Adjuster Report**:
- May need supplemental inspection if damage missed
- Don't sign anything releasing rights prematurely
- Get multiple opinions if unsure

**Step 5: Select Contractor and Begin Work**:
- Choose experienced local contractor
- Verify insurance claim experience
- Get everything in writing
- Don't pay until work complete and inspected

### Texas Insurance Rights

**Important Protections**:
- You choose the contractor (not insurance company)
- You're entitled to full replacement cost (if policy includes)
- Depreciation should be refunded after completion
- Code upgrades may be covered
- You can dispute insufficient settlements

**Red Flags**:
- Contractors who 'work with insurance' and inflate prices
- Offering to pay your deductible (illegal)
- Requiring assignment of benefits
- Pressure to sign before reading everything
