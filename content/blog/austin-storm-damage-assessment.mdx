## Identifying Different Types of Storm Damage

### Hail Damage Recognition

**Shingle Damage Indicators**:
- **Granule Loss**: Dark or shiny spots where protective granules are missing
- **Impact Marks**: Visible dents, bruises, or divots in shingles
- **Fractured Shingles**: Cracks or splits in roofing material
- **Exposed Asphalt**: Black substrate showing through where granules knocked off
- **Random Distribution**: Damage in irregular patterns (different from normal aging)
- **Fiberglass Matting**: Visible in severe cases where shingle integrity compromised

**Roof Component Damage**:
- Dented metal gutters and downspouts
- Damaged gutter guards or leaf screens
- Dented or cracked roof vents
- Compromised chimney caps and flashing
- Damaged skylight frames or glazing
- Cracked pipe boot seals
- Dented ridge vent caps

**Property-Wide Storm Indicators**:
- Dented air conditioning condenser units
- Damaged window screens or frames
- Impact marks on siding or painted surfaces
- Fence damage or deck impact marks
- Outdoor lighting fixture damage
- Mailbox or landscape feature damage
- Vehicle damage (if exposed during storm)

### Wind Damage Identification

**Direct Wind Damage**:
- Missing shingles or shingle tabs
- Lifted or curled shingle edges
- Torn or ripped shingles exposing underlayment
- Missing or displaced ridge caps
- Damaged starter shingles at roof edges
- Loose or detached flashing

**Secondary Wind Damage**:
- Tree branches on roof
- Debris lodged in valleys or gutters
- Damaged or detached gutters
- Torn soffit or fascia
- Displaced or broken trim
- Sagging roof sections

### Water Intrusion from Storm Damage

**Interior Warning Signs**:
- Ceiling stains or water spots
- Wall discoloration near roofline
- Attic moisture or dampness
- Active dripping during rain
- Mold or mildew odor
- Peeling interior paint

**Exterior Water Damage**:
- Staining on exterior walls
- Compromised valley flashing
- Damaged or missing drip edge
- Water pooling on roof (indicates drainage issues)
- Moss or algae (chronic moisture retention)

## Safe Self-Assessment Procedures

### Ground-Level Inspection Safety

**Critical Safety Rules**:
- NEVER climb on your roof after a storm
- Don't use ladders without proper training and equipment
- Stay clear of downed power lines
- Watch for loose debris that could fall
- Professional assessment is safer and more comprehensive

**What You Can Safely Check**:

**1. Complete Exterior Walk-Around**
- Photograph all sides of your home from ground level
- Use binoculars to inspect roof surface from safe distance
- Check gutters for visible damage or debris
- Look for shingle pieces in yard or on ground
- Inspect all exterior property components
- Note any immediate safety concerns

**2. Gutter System Inspection**
- Look for dents, dings, or deformation in metal gutters
- Check for loose or separated sections
- Note bent or damaged downspouts
- Look for granule accumulation in gutters
- Check downspout discharge areas for debris

**3. Exterior Component Assessment**
- AC condenser unit for hail dents
- Window screens for tears or holes
- Painted surfaces for impact damage
- Siding for cracks or displacement
- Outdoor fixtures and equipment
- Fence and deck structures

**4. Yard and Ground Check**
- Photograph any hail stones with size reference (use ruler or quarter)
- Note location and size of fallen branches
- Look for roofing granules on driveway or walkways
- Check for pieces of shingles or roofing material
- Document all storm debris

### Interior Assessment

**Attic Inspection** (If Safely Accessible):
- Look for light penetrating through roof
- Check rafters for water staining
- Feel insulation for dampness or saturation
- Note any active leaks or dripping
- Check for musty or moldy odors
- Inspect attic ventilation components

**Living Area Inspection**:
- Check all ceilings for stains or discoloration
- Examine walls near roofline for water damage
- Look for new cracks or structural concerns
- Note any areas that dripped during storm
- Monitor during next rain event

### Written Storm Documentation

**Create Detailed Storm Log**:

**Event Information**:
- Date and time storm occurred
- Duration of severe weather
- Hail size (from observation or weather reports)
- Wind speed estimates
- Type of precipitation
- When damage was discovered

**Damage Details**:
- Specific items damaged (roof, gutters, AC, etc.)
- Location on property
- Severity assessment
- Whether damage is functional or cosmetic
- Any immediate safety concerns
- Interior vs. exterior damage

**Supporting Documentation**:
- National Weather Service reports for your area
- Local news coverage of storm
- Neighbor reports or observations
- Prior roof inspection reports
- Maintenance records
- Property photos from before storm

## Understanding Next Steps After Assessment

### If Damage is Confirmed

**1. Review Professional Assessment**
- Understand scope and severity of damage
- Ask questions about findings
- Discuss urgency and recommended timeline
- Review repair vs. replacement options
- Get clarification on any technical terms

**2. Contact Your Insurance Company**
- Report damage promptly per policy requirements
- Provide storm date and basic description
- Request adjuster inspection
- Have your professional inspection report ready
- Keep claim number and adjuster contact info

**3. Mitigate Further Damage**
- Arrange emergency tarping if actively leaking
- Document all temporary protective measures with photos
- Keep receipts for emergency services
- Don't make permanent repairs before adjuster inspection
- Continue monitoring condition

**4. Gather Repair Estimates**
- Get detailed written scope from licensed contractors
- Ensure estimates address all identified damage
- Compare proposals and credentials
- Verify licensing and insurance
- Keep comprehensive documentation

## Preparing for Austin's Storm Season

### Proactive Preparation

**Before Severe Weather Season**:
- Schedule annual professional roof inspection
- Clean gutters and trim overhanging tree branches
- Secure or store loose outdoor items and furniture
- Document current roof condition with dated photos
- Review homeowners insurance policy coverage
- Update emergency contact list
- Check that roof vents and flashing are secure

**Emergency Preparedness Kit**:
- Flashlight with fresh batteries
- Tarps and fastening materials (for emergencies)
- Camera or smartphone with charged battery
- Buckets and towels for potential leaks
- Copy of insurance policy and important documents
- Contractor and insurance contact numbers
- First aid kit and emergency supplies

### Post-Storm Actions

**Immediate Response** (Within 24 Hours):
- Safely inspect property from ground level only
- Take comprehensive photographs and video
- Document date, time, and storm characteristics
- Check for interior leaks or water damage
- Note hail size if stones are still visible
- Check with neighbors about their observations

**Follow-Up Actions** (Within Week):
- Contact professional inspector if damage suspected
- File insurance claim if professional confirms damage
- Arrange emergency repairs if necessary (with full documentation)
- Monitor for developing issues (leaks often appear days later)
- Save all receipts, reports, and communications
- Maintain detailed chronological record
