## Recognizing Different Types of Storm Damage

### Hail Damage Identification

**Roof Surface Damage**:
- **Granule Loss**: Dark or shiny spots where protective granules are knocked off
- **Impact Dents**: Visible divots or bruising on shingles
- **Cracked Shingles**: Fractures or splits in roofing material
- **Exposed Substrate**: Black asphalt showing through where granules are missing
- **Random Pattern**: Damage appears in irregular patterns (vs. uniform aging)

**Accessory Damage**:
- Metal gutters with visible dents or dings
- Damaged gutter guards or screens
- Dented roof vents or turbines
- Compromised chimney flashing
- Damaged skylight frames
- Cracked or broken pipe boots

**Property-Wide Indicators**:
- Dented or damaged AC condenser unit
- Window screen holes or tears
- Siding damage or paint chips
- Fence or deck impact marks
- Damaged outdoor light fixtures
- Vehicle damage (if parked outside)

### Wind Damage Indicators

**Shingle Damage**:
- Missing shingle tabs or entire shingles
- Lifted or curled shingle edges
- Torn or ripped shingles
- Exposed underlayment (black tar paper)
- Displaced ridge caps
- Loose or missing starter strips

**Structural Concerns**:
- Damaged or detached gutters
- Compromised fascia boards
- Torn soffit panels
- Fallen tree branches on roof
- Debris in valleys or against chimneys
- Sagging or uneven roof sections

### Water Damage from Storms

**Interior Signs**:
- Ceiling stains or discoloration
- Water spots on walls
- Attic moisture or dampness
- Mold or mildew smell
- Peeling paint near roofline
- Dripping or active leaks during rain

**Exterior Signs**:
- Water staining on siding
- Damaged or missing drip edge
- Compromised valley flashing
- Moss or algae growth (indicates chronic moisture)

## Safe Self-Assessment Methods

### Ground-Level Inspection

**Safety First**: NEVER climb on your roof. Professional assessment is safer and more thorough.

**What You Can Check Safely**:

**1. Complete Property Walk-Around**
- Photograph all four sides of your home
- Use binoculars to inspect shingles from different angles
- Check gutters for dents or damage
- Look for shingle pieces or debris in yard
- Inspect all ground-level property components

**2. Gutter and Downspout Inspection**
- Look for visible dents or dings in metal
- Check for loose or detached sections
- Note any bending or warping
- Inspect connections and fasteners
- Look for granules collecting in gutters

**3. Exterior Component Check**
- Examine AC unit condenser for hail dents
- Check window screens for holes or tears
- Inspect painted surfaces for impact marks
- Look at siding for cracks or damage
- Check outdoor lights and fixtures

**4. Yard and Landscape Review**
- Photograph any hail stones with size reference (ruler, quarter)
- Note fallen branches or tree damage
- Look for shingle granules on ground or driveway
- Check for pieces of roofing material
- Document storm debris

### Interior Assessment

**Attic Inspection** (If Safely Accessible):
- Look for daylight showing through roof
- Check for water stains on rafters
- Feel insulation for dampness
- Look for active leaks or drips
- Note any musty odors

**Living Space Check**:
- Inspect ceilings in all rooms
- Look for water stains or discoloration
- Check walls near roofline
- Note any new cracks or damage
- Monitor for leaks during next rain

**Create a Storm Damage Log**:

**Date & Time Information**:
- When storm occurred
- When you discovered damage
- Weather conditions during storm
- Hail size (from weather reports or observation)

**Damage Description**:
- Specific items damaged
- Location of damage on property
- Severity assessment
- Any immediate concerns (leaks, safety)

**Reference Information**:
- National Weather Service storm reports
- Local news coverage
- Neighbor damage reports
- Previous roof inspection dates

## Understanding the Next Steps

### If Damage is Identified

**1. Review Professional Findings**
- Understand scope and severity
- Ask questions about recommendations
- Discuss urgency and timeline
- Get clarification on repair options

**2. Contact Insurance Company**
- Report damage promptly (most policies require timely notification)
- Provide storm date and basic damage description
- Request adjuster inspection
- Have your documentation organized

**3. Prevent Additional Damage**
- Emergency tarping if actively leaking (document with photos)
- Keep receipts for emergency services
- Don't make permanent repairs before adjuster visits
- Continue monitoring for changes

**4. Obtain Written Estimates**
- Get detailed scope from licensed contractors
- Ensure estimates cover all necessary work
- Compare proposals carefully
- Keep all documentation organized

### If No Damage is Found

**Benefits of Professional Inspection**:
- Peace of mind about roof condition
- Baseline documentation for future reference
- Opportunity to discuss maintenance needs
- Understanding of roof's remaining lifespan
- Proactive identification of minor issues

**Ongoing Maintenance**:
- Schedule regular inspections (annually recommended)
- Address minor issues promptly
- Keep gutters clean and clear
- Monitor after future severe weather
- Maintain documentation of roof condition

## Preparing for Round Rock Storm Season

### Before Severe Weather

**Proactive Preparation**:
- Schedule annual professional inspection
- Clean gutters and trim overhanging branches
- Secure loose outdoor items and furniture
- Document current roof condition with photos
- Review insurance policy coverage details
- Have emergency contact numbers accessible

**Emergency Supplies**:
- Flashlight with fresh batteries
- Tarps and basic fasteners (for emergencies)
- Camera or smartphone for documentation
- Bucket and towels (for potential leaks)
- Insurance policy and important documents

### After the Storm

**Immediate Actions** (First 24 Hours):
- Safely inspect from ground level
- Take comprehensive photographs
- Document date, time, and storm severity
- Check for interior leaks or damage
- Note hail size if possible
- Check on neighbors (widespread damage indicator)

**Within Days**:
- Contact professional inspector if damage suspected
- File insurance claim if damage confirmed
- Schedule emergency repairs if needed (with documentation)
- Continue monitoring for developing issues
- Save all receipts and documentation
- Keep detailed records of all communications
