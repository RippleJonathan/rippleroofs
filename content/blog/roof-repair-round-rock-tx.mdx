## A Round Rock Roof Repair Done Right

When a homeowner in Round Rock noticed missing shingles and a small water stain creeping across their ceiling after a spring storm, they called Ripple Roofing & Construction. What they needed wasn't a full roof replacement—just an honest, skilled repair to stop the leak and protect the rest of the roof. That's exactly what our crew delivered.

### The Situation

This home, tucked into one of Round Rock's established neighborhoods, had a shingle roof that was otherwise in decent shape. But a run of high winds and hail had loosened and cracked several shingles near a valley, letting water sneak underneath the roofing system. Left alone, a problem like this only gets worse—wood decking can rot, insulation gets ruined, and a simple fix turns into a much bigger project.

### What Our Crew Did

Our team inspected the entire roof first, not just the obvious trouble spot. That's a habit we've built over years of doing this work: storm damage rarely shows up in just one place. We found the damaged shingles, checked the flashing and underlayment around the valley, and confirmed the decking underneath was still solid.

From there, the repair itself was straightforward for our crew: remove the compromised shingles, replace the damaged section of underlayment, and install new shingles that match the existing roofline. We take care to blend repairs so they look like part of the original roof, not a patch job that stands out.

You can see the before-and-after photos below—notice how the repaired section sits flush and matches the surrounding shingles in color and pattern.

### Why It Matters

A roof repair done correctly isn't just about stopping a leak today. It's about making sure the fix holds up through the next storm, and the one after that. As a CertainTeed ShingleMaster Premier Contractor, we hold ourselves to a standard that goes beyond just covering a hole—we're looking at the whole system: shingles, underlayment, flashing, and ventilation, so small problems don't turn into big ones.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How do I know if I need a roof repair or a full roof replacement?

It depends on the extent and location of the damage. If the issue is isolated—like a section of shingles damaged by storms or wind—a repair is often enough, especially if the rest of the roof and the decking underneath are still in good shape. We inspect the whole roof before recommending anything, so you get an honest answer either way.

### How long does a typical roof repair take?

Most localized repairs, like the one shown here, can be completed in a single day. Larger or more complex repairs may take longer, depending on how much of the roof is affected and what we find once we get up there.

### Do you serve areas outside of Round Rock?

Yes. We regularly work in Georgetown, Austin, Elgin, Copperas Cove, and other nearby Central Texas communities. Give us a call and let us know where you're located.

### What should I do if I notice missing or damaged shingles after a storm?
