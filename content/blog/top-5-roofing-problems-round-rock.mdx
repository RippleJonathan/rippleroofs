## 1. Hail Damage: Round Rock's #1 Roof Enemy

**The Problem:**  
Round Rock sits in "Hail Alley," experiencing some of Texas's most severe hailstorms. Golf ball-sized hail is common, especially during spring and early summer. Even seemingly minor hail can cause:

- Granule loss on shingles (exposing the asphalt underneath)
- Dented or cracked shingles
- Compromised flashing around vents and chimneys
- Hidden damage that leads to leaks months later

**The Solution:**  
After any significant hail event:
1. Schedule a professional inspection within 48 hours
2. Document all damage with photos for insurance
3. File your claim promptly (most policies cover hail damage)
4. Choose impact-resistant Class 4 shingles for replacement

**Pro Tip:** Many Round Rock insurance companies offer premium discounts for Class 4 impact-resistant roofing. Ask us about CertainTeed's IR (Impact Resistant) shingles—they're designed for Texas weather.

## 2. Extreme Heat and UV Degradation

**The Problem:**  
Round Rock summers regularly exceed 100°F, with intense UV exposure accelerating roof aging. This causes:

- Shingle brittleness and cracking
- Accelerated granule loss
- Warping and curling shingles
- Premature roof failure (15-20 years instead of 25-30)
- Higher cooling costs due to heat absorption

**The Solution:**  
- Install light-colored or reflective shingles to reduce heat absorption
- Ensure proper attic ventilation (ridge vents + soffit vents)
- Consider cool roof coatings for extended lifespan
- Schedule bi-annual inspections to catch early signs of UV damage

**Local Insight:** Round Rock's average summer temperature is 5-10°F hotter than coastal areas, making UV-resistant materials essential.

## 3. Wind Damage from Severe Storms

**The Problem:**  
Round Rock experiences frequent severe thunderstorms with wind gusts exceeding 60 mph. High winds cause:

- Lifted or missing shingles
- Exposed underlayment vulnerable to leaks
- Damaged flashing and trim
- Debris impact damage from flying branches

**The Solution:**  
- Use wind-resistant shingles rated for 130+ mph winds
- Proper installation with 6-nail patterns (not just 4-nail)
- Regular inspections after storm events
- Trim overhanging tree branches before storm season

**Round Rock Reality:** Dell Diamond area and neighborhoods near the IKEA corridor are particularly exposed to straight-line winds due to open terrain.

## 4. Poor Installation from Unlicensed Contractors

**The Problem:**  
Round Rock's rapid growth has attracted fly-by-night roofing contractors offering lowball bids. Poor installation leads to:

- Voided manufacturer warranties
- Leaks at valleys, flashing, and penetrations
- Need for complete re-roof within 5-10 years

**Common Installation Mistakes We See:**

## 5. Inadequate Attic Ventilation

**The Problem:**  
Many Round Rock homes—especially older builds in Heritage Park, Forest Creek, and Settler's Ridge—have insufficient attic ventilation. This causes:

- Excessive heat buildup (attics can reach 150°F+)
- Shortened shingle lifespan from underside heat damage
- Ice damming in rare winter freezes
- Mold and mildew growth
- Higher energy bills (AC works harder)

**Signs of Poor Ventilation:**
- Hot ceilings in summer
- Mold or mildew in attic spaces
- Rusted nails or metal in attic
- Warped or wavy roof decking
- Peeling exterior paint near roof line

**The Solution:**  
Proper ventilation requires balanced intake and exhaust:
- **Intake:** Continuous soffit vents or gable vents
- **Exhaust:** Ridge vents, powered attic fans, or turbine vents
- **Rule of Thumb:** 1 sq ft of ventilation per 150 sq ft of attic space

**Round Rock Bonus Tip:** With our extreme summer heat, consider solar-powered attic fans. They pay for themselves in energy savings within 2-3 years.
