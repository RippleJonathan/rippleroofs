## Common Roofing Problems in Lakeway Luxury Homes

### 1. Storm Damage in Exposed Locations

**Lakeway's Exposure**:
- Hilltop and lakefront locations face stronger winds
- Hail events 2-4 times per year in Central Texas
- Lightning strikes on elevated properties

**Prevention**:
- Class 4 impact-resistant materials (mandatory recommendation)
- High wind ratings (130 mph+ for exposed locations)
- Lightning protection systems for tall structures
- Regular post-storm inspections

### 2. Humidity and Moisture (Lakefront Properties)

**Lake Travis Effect**:
- Higher humidity near water
- Morning dew and moisture
- Potential for mold/mildew

**Solutions**:
- Enhanced ventilation systems
- Moisture-resistant underlayment
- Corrosion-resistant fasteners (stainless steel)
- Regular inspections for moisture issues

### 3. Oak Tree Management

**Lakeway's Oak Canopy**:
- Beautiful live oaks add property value
- But create roof maintenance needs
- Limbs can cause major damage in storms

**Best Practices**:
- Annual professional arborist assessment
- Trim branches 10+ feet from roof
- Remove dead/diseased trees promptly
- Balance preservation with protection

### 4. Architectural Complexity Challenges

**Custom Design Issues**:
- Multiple valleys (leak-prone)
- Complex flashing requirements
- Steep pitches (water velocity)
- Custom details require expert execution

**Solutions**:
- Hire only experienced luxury contractors
- Use premium flashing materials
- Enhanced underlayment in valleys
- Detailed quality control during installation

### 5. UV and Heat Degradation

**Hill Country Sun**:
- 310+ sunny days per year
- Roof surface temps 150-170¬∞F+
- Even premium materials deteriorate

**Prevention**:
- Light-colored materials (reflect heat)
- Cool roof technology
- Proper ventilation (reduces attic temps)
- Regular maintenance extends life
