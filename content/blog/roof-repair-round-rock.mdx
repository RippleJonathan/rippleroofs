### What Roof Repair Covers

Roof repair is a broad category, and understanding what falls under it can help you know what to expect:

- **Leaks** — Water stains on ceilings or walls almost always trace back to a specific point of failure on the roof, whether it's a cracked pipe boot, worn sealant, or a gap in the underlayment.
- **Missing or damaged shingles** — Wind and hail regularly knock shingles loose or crack them outright, exposing the roof deck underneath.
- **Flashing issues** — The metal flashing around chimneys, skylights, and roof valleys is a common failure point, especially on older homes.
- **Storm damage** — Hail bruising, wind-lifted shingles, and impact marks from falling branches all fall under storm damage repair, one of the most common calls we get in Central Texas.

## Common Signs Your Central Texas Roof Needs Repair

Most roof problems give off warning signs well before they turn into a bigger headache. Here's what we tell homeowners to watch for:

- Water spots or discoloration on interior ceilings, especially after heavy rain
- Shingles that are curling, cracked, or completely missing
- Granules collecting in gutters or at the base of downspouts
- Visible dents or bruising on shingles after a hailstorm
- Daylight visible through the attic roof boards
- Sagging sections of the roofline
- Rising energy bills, which can point to ventilation or insulation gaps caused by roof damage

If you've noticed any of these, it's worth having a professional take a look before the next storm rolls through. Our [roof inspection checklist](https://www.rippleroofs.com/resources/roof-inspection-checklist) is a helpful starting point if you want to do a quick check yourself first.

### Repair or Replace? Knowing the Difference

Not every damaged roof needs a full replacement, and we'll never push you toward a bigger job than you actually need. As a rough guide, repair usually makes sense when damage is isolated to a small area and the rest of the roof is in solid shape. Replacement becomes the smarter option when a roof is nearing the end of its expected lifespan, has widespread storm damage, or has been patched multiple times already. We break this down further in our [repair vs. replacement guide](https://www.rippleroofs.com/blog/roof-replacement-vs-repair-guide).
